Syllabus: Introduction to Microsoft Teams

Workplace Productivity & Collaboration DRAFT

Course Description

Introduction to Microsoft Teams teaches learners to use Teams as a comprehensive collaboration platform within the Microsoft 365 ecosystem. Students will understand Teams fundamentals including chat, video meetings, and file sharing, explore integration with other Microsoft 365 tools, create and structure Teams and Channels effectively, and manage permissions and settings for team collaboration. This course enables professionals to leverage Microsoft Teams for workplace communication and project collaboration.

Summative Assessment: Team Organization and Collaboration Project

Students will create and organize a functional Microsoft Teams workspace. The assessment requires creation of a Team with appropriate structure, multiple Channels organized by purpose, proper permission configuration, file sharing setup, and demonstration of basic communication features. Learners must show understanding of best practices for team organization and collaboration.

Learning Outcomes

  1. Discuss what Microsoft Teams is and its core functionality
  2. Explain how Microsoft Teams fits within the Microsoft 365 ecosystem
  3. Navigate the Teams interface effectively
  4. Create and customize Teams
  5. Create, organize, and manage Channels
  6. Configure Teams and Channel settings
  7. Manage permissions and access control
  8. Use chat for team and direct communication
  9. Schedule and conduct video meetings
  10. Share and collaborate on files within Teams
  11. Integrate other Microsoft 365 applications
  12. Apply best practices for team organization
