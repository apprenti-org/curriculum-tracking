Syllabus: Student Onboarding

Training Operations 8 hours DRAFT

Course Description

Student Onboarding is a required self-paced course designed to prepare all incoming apprentices for success in their respective programs. The course establishes learning philosophy including problem-based learning, experiential learning, growth mindset, and self-efficacy. Students explore effective learning strategies, understand the student-instructor relationship, set up required tools and accounts, acknowledge policies, and build readiness for intensive technical training. This course ensures learners begin their programs with clear expectations and necessary foundation.

Summative Assessment: Onboarding Verification and Readiness Check

Students will complete quizzes assessing understanding of learning philosophy and success strategies, verify completion of tool setup and account creation, and demonstrate readiness for their specific program. The assessment ensures learners have foundational knowledge of expectations and are prepared to engage effectively in subsequent courses.

Learning Outcomes

  1. Understand the RTI Academy program structure and intensity
  2. Apply growth mindset principles to learning
  3. Build self-efficacy and confidence as learners
  4. Understand and apply the Two-Week Rule (comfort with uncertainty)
  5. Distinguish between brain memory and muscle memory
  6. Approach course material effectively
  7. Explain problems and understand concepts deeply
  8. Propose solutions and incorporate feedback
  9. Ask for help effectively and appropriately
  10. Apply systems-level thinking to problems
  11. Set up required tools and accounts
  12. Understand student handbook and policies
  13. Understand the AI and Technology Use Policy
  14. Prepare for success in their specific program

Course Outline

1 Course Introduction & Program Overview 1 hours
  • Welcome and course description
  • Program structure overview (modules, courses, intensity)
  • Learning expectations and intensity preparation
  • Support systems available
  • Instructor mentorship model

  • Know what to expect from instructors and support systems
  • Feel welcomed to the learning community
2 How to Be Successful — Learning Philosophy (1.5 Hours)
  • Problem-Based Learning (learning through problem-solving)
  • Experiential Learning (learning through doing)
  • Growth Mindset (abilities developed through effort)
  • Self-Efficacy (confidence in ability to succeed)
  • The Two-Week Rule (normal to feel uncomfortable early on)
  • Brain Memory vs Muscle Memory (theory vs practice)
  • Understand learning science principles
  • Adopt a growth mindset
  • Believe in ability to learn and improve
3 How to Learn — Skills & Strategies (1.5 Hours)
  • How to approach and work with course material
  • Reading, thinking, and comprehension strategies
  • How to explain and understand problems
  • Critical thinking and problem analysis
  • How to propose solutions and refine them
  • How to incorporate feedback effectively
  • How and when to ask for help
  • Asking specific, clear questions
  • Systems-level thinking
  • The Engineer's Notebook (documentation and reflection)
  • Know effective learning strategies
  • Understand when and how to seek help
  • Be able to ask clarifying questions
  • Document learning and reasoning
4 Instructor-Student Relationship & Feedback Model 1 hours
  • The instructor role: mentor and guide, not content deliverer
  • The learning cycle: Read → Think → Sketch → Seek Feedback → Experiment
  • Focus on thought process and understanding over right answers
  • Treating solutions as experiments and learning opportunities
  • Responding to feedback constructively
  • Understand instructor role and expectations
  • Know how to interact with instructors effectively
  • Value feedback as learning opportunity
