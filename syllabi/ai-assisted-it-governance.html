Learning Outcomes

  1. Identify the characteristics of policy and protocol documents that inform AI-assisted drafting approaches
  2. Apply prompt strategies for drafting policy content with appropriate tone, regulatory context, and organizational constraints
  3. Use AI to analyze existing policy documents for gaps, redundancies, and outdated provisions
  4. Apply iterative feedback prompts to improve the quality of AI-drafted policy language
  5. Evaluate AI-generated policy text using defined human review checkpoints
  6. Describe common sources of inconsistency in multi-document technical environments
  7. Apply AI-assisted review workflows to identify contradictions, terminology drift, and alignment failures across documents
  8. Construct prompts for systematic consistency checks using terminology standards and reference documents
  9. Interpret structured AI-generated consistency audit output and prioritize findings for resolution
  10. Apply human oversight practices to validate and act on AI-identified inconsistencies
  11. Describe the key components of responsible AI frameworks applicable to IT governance workflows
  12. Apply AI to compliance auditing against regulatory requirements such as GLBA, Reg E, and FCRA
  13. Use AI to draft and review security governance documentation and access management procedures
  14. Apply AI adherence checks to verify that policy content aligns with current regulatory and security standards
  15. Identify categories of compliance and governance decisions that require human authority rather than AI judgment
  16. Identify ethical risks in AI-assisted decisions, including bias, fairness concerns, and unintended consequences
  17. Apply AI to business risk analysis: identification, categorization, likelihood assessment, and mitigation strategy development
  18. Use AI to structure and populate Business Continuity Plan frameworks from existing operational context
  19. Evaluate the accuracy and reliability of AI-generated risk analysis output
  20. Apply documentation discipline to record AI-generated risk assessments and the human decisions made in response

Course Outline

1 AI-Assisted IT Governance 4 hours
Lesson 1: AI for Policy Document Drafting and Text Analysis (1h)
  • Characteristics of policy and protocol documents: structure, precision requirements, compliance language, and audience considerations
  • Using AI to analyze existing policy documents: identifying gaps, redundancies, and outdated provisions
  • Prompt strategies for drafting policy content: specifying jurisdiction, organizational constraints, and regulatory context
  • Iterative refinement: using targeted feedback prompts to improve AI-drafted policy language
  • Validating AI-generated policy text: human review checkpoints and escalation criteria
Lesson 2: Reviewing and Ensuring Consistency in Technical Documents (1h)
  • Multi-document consistency challenges: terminology drift, conflicting clauses, version gaps, and cross-reference errors
  • AI-assisted review workflows: cross-referencing documents for contradictions, alignment failures, and naming inconsistencies
  • Prompt strategies for consistency checks: specifying terminology standards, style guides, and reference documents
  • Structured output patterns: using AI to produce consistency audit reports with actionable findings
  • Human oversight of AI review output: interpreting, prioritizing, and acting on AI-identified inconsistencies
Lesson 3: Applying Responsible AI Frameworks for Compliance and Security Governance (1h)
  • Responsible AI frameworks: key components of structured governance approaches for AI-assisted work
  • Compliance auditing with AI: reviewing content and outputs against regulatory requirements (e.g., GLBA, Reg E, FCRA)
  • Security governance documentation: using AI to draft and review security policies, controls documentation, and access management procedures
  • Adherence checks: verifying that AI-assisted policy content aligns with current regulatory and security standards
  • Scope limits: categories of compliance and governance decisions requiring human authority and review
Lesson 4: Ethical and Business Risk Analysis with AI (1h)
  • Ethical risk analysis: identifying bias, fairness concerns, and unintended consequences in AI-assisted decisions and outputs
  • Business risk analysis using AI: prompting for risk identification, categorization, likelihood, and mitigation strategies
  • Business Continuity Plan (BCP) drafting: using AI to structure and populate BCP frameworks from existing operational context
  • Evaluating AI risk analysis output: distinguishing well-grounded findings from plausible-but-unsupported claims
  • Documentation discipline: recording AI-generated risk assessments and the human decisions made in response
