Syllabus: LLM History & Capabilities Deep Dive

Cyber Developer Associate 8 hours CURRENT

Course Description

A foundational artificial-intelligence literacy course for the Cyber Developer Associate. Learners trace the development of AI and machine learning to the modern large language model, learn what these models can and cannot do and how they work under the hood, and study their characteristic failure modes. The course closes by turning those limitations into working practice: how to use AI assistants to write secure software with appropriate skepticism and verification.

Summative Assessment

A final cumulative quiz covering all five lessons, plus a per-lesson quiz and an analytical activity in each lesson (capability-vs-misconception sorting, failure-mode spotting, and reviewing AI-generated code for security issues). There is no capstone; assessment is carried by the quizzes and activities.

Learning Outcomes

  1. Trace the development of artificial intelligence and machine learning from early symbolic systems to modern large language models, and explain how the major paradigm shifts changed what machines could do.
  2. Describe what large language models can and cannot do, distinguishing genuine capabilities from common misconceptions.
  3. Explain how a large language model processes language — tokens, context windows, and inference — well enough to predict why it behaves as it does.
  4. Identify the characteristic failure modes of large language models — hallucination, drift, and boundary conditions — and recognize when an output is likely to be unreliable.
  5. Assess the implications of large language model limitations for secure software development, and apply that judgment when using an AI assistant to produce code.

Course Outline

1 Foundations and How LLMs Work 4 hours
Lesson: 1: A Brief History of AI and Machine Learning (1.5h)
  • From symbolic AI and expert systems to statistical machine learning
  • The shift to neural networks and deep learning
  • The transformer breakthrough and the rise of large language models
  • Why this history matters: each shift changed what could be automated, and what could go wrong
Lesson: 2: What LLMs Can and Cannot Do (1.5h)
  • Genuine capabilities: language generation, summarization, translation, code assistance
  • Hard limits: no ground truth, no real-time knowledge, no reasoning guarantees
  • Capability vs. misconception — separating what models do from what people assume they do
  • Setting realistic expectations for an AI assistant
Lesson: 3: How LLMs Work — Tokens, Context Windows, and Inference (1h)
  • Tokens: how text is broken into units a model processes
  • The context window: what the model can see at once, and what falls out of it
  • Inference: next-token prediction and why output is probabilistic, not retrieved
  • How these mechanics explain everyday model behavior
2 Failure Modes and Secure Implications 4 hours
Lesson: 4: LLM Failure Modes — Hallucination, Drift, and Boundary Conditions (2h)
  • Hallucination: confident, fluent, and wrong — why it happens
  • Drift: how output degrades across long contexts and conversations
  • Boundary conditions: prompts at the edges of training and context limits
  • Recognizing the signals that an output should not be trusted as-is
Lesson: 5: LLM Limitations and Secure Software Development (2h)
  • Why AI-generated code can be insecure, outdated, or subtly wrong
  • Treating model output as a draft to verify, never as authoritative
  • Where human review is non-negotiable: secrets, input validation, dependencies, logic
  • A habit of verification: turning the limitations from the rest of the course into working practice

Software Requirements

  • None — no software installation required; the course is conceptual and instructor-led.
