Learning Outcomes

  1. Identify the major categories of AI-enhanced ITSM tools and their primary use cases
  2. Evaluate AI tools against defined criteria: accuracy, integration requirements, auditability, and cost
  3. Describe how AI prioritization algorithms process and rank incoming tickets
  4. Explain the human oversight requirements and key risks in AI-assisted ticket management workflows (OJL 5.c)
  5. Explain why unstructured client communication creates consistency and processing challenges in ITSM workflows
  6. Design prompts to extract key triage fields from unstructured ticket text (OJL 5.c)
  7. Design prompts that produce professional, consistent ticket acknowledgment messages
  8. Apply an iterative refinement process to improve AI-generated ticket outputs
  9. Identify the required components of a structured resolution summary (OJL 5.d)
  10. Design prompts to generate structured resolution summaries from unstructured technician notes
  11. Describe how LLMs can be used to query and synthesize information from historical resolution data
  12. Apply fact-checking practices to verify AI-synthesized content against source records
  13. Identify situations in ITSM where technical documents require simplification for different audiences (OJL 5.d)
  14. Design prompts to rewrite or summarize complex technical content for a specified target audience
  15. Apply AI to generate knowledge base entries from resolved ticket data (OJL 5.d)
  16. Describe responsible use practices for AI in ITSM documentation workflows

Course Outline

1 AI-Assisted IT Service Management 4 hours
Lesson: 1: AI Tools for ITSM — Evaluation and Selection (1h)
  • Landscape of AI-enhanced ITSM tools: CRM systems, ticketing platforms (e.g., ServiceNow, Jira Service Management), and prioritization engines
  • Criteria for evaluating AI tools: accuracy, integration requirements, auditability, and cost
  • How AI prioritization algorithms work and how to manage AI-enhanced ticket queues
  • Risks and human oversight requirements: bias in prioritization, over-automation, escalation paths
  • Wrap-Up and Reflection (5 min)
Lesson: 2: Prompt Design for Ticket Triage and Acknowledgment (1h)
  • Why unstructured client emails and tickets are hard to process consistently
  • Prompt structures for extracting key fields from unstructured ticket text: category, urgency, affected system, and requested action
  • Designing prompts to generate professional, consistent ticket acknowledgment messages
  • Iterating prompts: identifying when outputs are vague, off-tone, or inaccurate and how to refine
  • Wrap-Up and Reflection (5 min)
Lesson: 3: Generating Structured Resolution Summaries and Querying Historical Notes (1h)
  • Components of a resolution summary: symptom, root cause, resolution steps, verification, and follow-up actions
  • Prompting AI to produce structured resolution summaries from unstructured technician notes
  • Using LLMs to query and synthesize information from a corpus of past resolution notes (retrieval patterns, semantic search concepts)
  • Accuracy and attribution: fact-checking AI-synthesized content against source records
  • Wrap-Up and Reflection (5 min)
Lesson: 4: Simplifying Complex Technical Documents with AI (1h)
  • When technical documents need simplification: runbooks for non-technical stakeholders, escalation guides, KB articles
  • Prompting AI to rewrite or summarize complex technical content for a specified audience
  • Generating knowledge base entries from resolved ticket data
  • Responsible use in ITSM: audit trails, human review gates, and attribution standards
  • Wrap-Up and Reflection (5 min)
