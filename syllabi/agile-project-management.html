Syllabus: Agile Project Management with Scrum

IT Business Analyst 40 hours DRAFT

Course Description

This course provides a comprehensive introduction to Agile project management using the Scrum framework. Students progress from foundational Agile principles through advanced Scrum topics, covering the Agile Manifesto, Scrum roles, ceremonies, and artifacts, as well as real-world implementation strategies, metrics, tooling, and scaling approaches. The course culminates in a capstone sprint simulation and certification preparation.

Students will gain hands-on experience facilitating Scrum events, managing backlogs, coaching teams, and applying Scrum in both standard and complex environments. This course prepares students for Scrum certification exams (PSM I, CSM, PMI-ACP) and equips them with the skills to lead Agile teams in professional settings.

Summative Assessment: Capstone Sprint Simulation

The capstone assessment requires students to form Scrum teams and execute a condensed sprint simulation. Students are assigned Scrum roles (Product Owner, Scrum Master, Developers) and given a fictional product brief. Teams must conduct Sprint Planning, execute a simulated Sprint with tasks and blockers, and complete a Sprint Review and Retrospective.

Students are assessed using a competency-based rubric measuring proficiency in Scrum role execution, ceremony facilitation, artifact management, and team collaboration. A comprehensive final quiz (15–20 questions) aligned to certification formats is also included.

Learning Outcomes

  1. Explain the Agile Manifesto, its four values, and twelve principles.
  2. Describe the history and evolution of Scrum within the Agile ecosystem.
  3. Compare Agile methodologies with traditional project management approaches.
  4. Identify the three Scrum pillars and five Scrum values.
  5. Map the Scrum process flow from Sprint Planning through Retrospective.
  6. Define the roles and responsibilities of the Product Owner, Scrum Master, and Development Team.
  7. Facilitate and participate effectively in all Scrum ceremonies.
  8. Create and manage Scrum artifacts: Product Backlog, Sprint Backlog, and Increment.
  9. Apply the Definition of Done to ensure quality and transparency.
  10. Prepare an organization for Scrum adoption and coach new Scrum teams.
  11. Use Scrum metrics (velocity, burn-down, burn-up) for inspection and adaptation.
  12. Adapt Scrum practices for complex, regulated, or scaled environments.

Course Outline

1 Introduction to Agile and Scrum 5 hours
Lesson: Agile Manifesto & Principles
  • Overview of the Agile Manifesto
  • The 4 values and 12 principles
  • Why Agile was created and what it replaced
  • Applying Agile values to real projects
Lesson: History and Evolution of Scrum
  • Origins of Scrum (Takeuchi & Nonaka, Sutherland, Schwaber)
  • Timeline of Scrum development
  • Key publications and industry adoption
  • Scrum's role in the Agile ecosystem
Lesson: Comparing Agile to Traditional Project Management
  • Waterfall vs Agile: life cycle, documentation, change management
  • Project Triangle: Scope, Cost, Time
  • Case Study: Waterfall vs Scrum project outcomes
Lesson: Agile Mindset & Team Culture
  • The "Agile mindset" and what it means in practice
  • Collaboration, trust, and self-organization
  • Psychological safety in Scrum teams
Activities
  • Interactive activity: "Agile Value Sorting"
  • Group Discussion: Which model fits which type of project?
  • Role-play scenarios: building or breaking Agile culture
2 Scrum Framework Overview 5 hours
Lesson: Scrum Pillars and Values
  • The 3 Pillars: Transparency, Inspection, Adaptation
  • The 5 Values: Commitment, Courage, Focus, Openness, Respect
  • Why values matter more than processes
Lesson: Scrum Process Flow
  • The Scrum lifecycle from Sprint Planning to Retrospective
  • Iterative vs Incremental development
  • Delivering value in every Sprint
Lesson: Key Differences: Scrum vs Other Agile Frameworks
  • Comparison: Kanban, XP, SAFe, Lean Software Development
  • Pros and cons of each model
  • Choosing the right framework for context
Lesson: Building Agile Teams
  • Scrum Team characteristics: cross-functional, self-organizing, collaborative
  • Ideal team size and composition
  • Tuckman's Team Development Model in Scrum context
Activities
  • Group activity: "Pillar vs Value Match"
  • Simulation: "A Day in the Life of a Scrum Team"
  • Team exercise: "Design Your Agile Dream Team"
3 Scrum Roles and Responsibilities 5 hours
Lesson: Product Owner — Vision, Backlog, Stakeholders
  • Product vision, backlog ownership, prioritization by business value
  • Writing user stories, stakeholder engagement, backlog refinement
Lesson: Scrum Master — Servant Leadership and Facilitation
  • Scrum Master as coach, facilitator, impediment remover, process guardian
  • Misconceptions about the Scrum Master role
Lesson: Development Team — Cross-functional Collaboration
  • Self-organizing, cross-functional teams (3–9 members)
  • Shared accountability and collective ownership
Lesson: Role Interactions and Accountability
  • How the three roles collaborate across Scrum events
  • Common anti-patterns and role conflicts
Activities
  • "Write a Product Vision Statement"
  • Role-play: "Scrum Master in Action"
  • Exercise: "Build a Dream Team"
  • Case study analysis: identifying and resolving role conflict
4 Scrum Ceremonies 5 hours
Lesson: Sprint Planning
  • Purpose, inputs, outputs, and format
  • Defining the Sprint Goal and Sprint Backlog
Lesson: Daily Scrum
  • 15-minute stand-up synchronization
  • Anti-patterns and tips for remote teams
Lesson: Sprint Review
  • Inspecting the increment with stakeholders
  • Emphasis on working product over presentations
Lesson: Sprint Retrospective
  • Inspecting team process and planning improvements
  • Techniques: Start/Stop/Continue, 4Ls, Sailboat, Mad/Sad/Glad
Activities
  • Simulate a Sprint Planning meeting
  • Roleplay: mock Daily Scrums
  • Design a Sprint Review agenda
  • Conduct a team Retrospective simulation
5 Scrum Artifacts 5 hours
Lesson: Product Backlog
  • Definition, purpose, and INVEST criteria
  • Backlog items: epics, user stories, bugs, spikes
  • Backlog Refinement techniques
Lesson: Sprint Backlog
  • Created during Sprint Planning, owned by the Development Team
  • Task breakdown, estimation, and burn-down charts
Lesson: Increment and Definition of Done
  • The Increment as a potentially shippable product
  • Definition of Done vs Acceptance Criteria
Lesson: Artifact Transparency and Refinement
  • Maintaining clear, visible, and accessible artifacts
  • Tools overview: Jira, Trello, Azure DevOps, ClickUp
  • Artifact anti-patterns and peer review
Activities
  • Refine a messy product backlog
  • Group activity: Create a Sprint Backlog
  • Write a Definition of Done
  • Hands-on: Explore artifact tracking tools
6 Implementing Scrum in a Team 5 hours
Lesson: Preparing an Organization for Scrum
  • Organizational mindset shift and readiness assessment
  • Management's role in supporting self-organizing teams
Lesson: Forming and Coaching Scrum Teams
  • Team formation, onboarding, and coaching strategies
  • Stages of team maturity (Tuckman model)
Lesson: Managing Stakeholders and Business Expectations
  • Stakeholder mapping, communication strategy, and transparent reporting
Lesson: Scaling Scrum with Multiple Teams
  • Scaling frameworks: Nexus, Scrum@Scale, SAFe, LeSS
  • Cross-team coordination and dependency management
Activities
  • Scrum Readiness Assessment
  • Workshop: Design a team launch plan
  • Write a stakeholder communication plan
  • Create a multi-team coordination plan
7 Advanced Scrum Topics 5 hours
Lesson: Metrics in Scrum — Velocity, Burn-down, Burn-up
  • Understanding and interpreting Scrum metrics
  • Cumulative Flow Diagrams
  • Misuse of metrics: vanity vs value
Lesson: Managing Technical Debt and Continuous Improvement
  • Technical debt causes, consequences, and management strategies
  • Continuous improvement through Kaizen and retrospectives
Lesson: Agile Tools (Jira, Trello, Azure Boards, etc.)
  • Tool comparison, Scrum board setup, automations and reports
Lesson: Adapting Scrum in Complex or Regulated Environments
  • Strategies for regulated industries
  • Hybrid models and risk management in Scrum
Activities
  • Interpret sample Sprint data
  • Workshop: Create a technical debt reduction plan
  • Hands-on lab: Create a Scrum board
  • Scenario activity: Plan a Sprint for a regulated project
8 Capstone Project and Certification Prep 5 hours
Lesson: Review of Core Concepts and Group Retrospective
  • Facilitated recap of all course topics
  • "Scrum Jeopardy" competitive review
Lesson: Final Capstone Sprint Simulation
  • Full sprint simulation with assigned Scrum roles
  • Sprint Planning, execution, Review, and Retrospective
Lesson: Troubleshooting Common Scrum Issues
  • Diagnosing Scrum-but, role confusion, poor backlog management
  • "Scrum Clinic" coaching exercise
Lesson: Final Quiz, Peer Review, and Certification Readiness
  • Comprehensive final quiz (15–20 questions)
  • Certification overview: PSM, CSM, PMI-ACP
  • Exam tips, prep strategies, and free resources

Software Requirements

  • No specific software required
  • Recommended: Access to an Agile project management tool (Jira, Trello, Azure DevOps, or ClickUp) for hands-on exercises
