Syllabus: Business Analysis Fundamentals

IT Business Analyst 120 hours DRAFT

Course Description

This course offers a comprehensive introduction to the field of business analysis, equipping learners with the foundational knowledge, tools, and techniques needed to perform effective analysis across strategic, tactical, and operational levels. Students will explore the core responsibilities of a business analyst, key concepts and terminology, and the structure of the BABOK® framework. Through detailed modules on planning, stakeholder collaboration, elicitation, requirements management, and solution evaluation, participants will gain hands-on experience in tools like RACI matrices, BPMN, use cases, and traceability matrices. The course culminates in a capstone project where students apply the full business analysis lifecycle—from stakeholder engagement and elicitation to solution recommendation and presentation—preparing them for real-world analysis roles in diverse business environments.

Summative Assessment: Capstone Project

The Capstone Project simulates a real-world consulting engagement, guiding students through the complete business analysis lifecycle—from stakeholder engagement to final solution recommendation and presentation. Acting as business analysts, learners apply tools and techniques such as stakeholder mapping, elicitation planning, process modeling, solution evaluation, and research integration to address a business challenge in a chosen scenario. This project reinforces core skills in communication, critical thinking, documentation, and visual modeling, culminating in a professional-grade presentation and supporting documentation package. It provides a practical opportunity to demonstrate mastery of business analysis practices in a structured, client-facing context.

Learning Outcomes

  1. Define business analysis and identify the roles and responsibilities of a business analyst
  2. Apply the BABOK® framework to guide business analysis activities
  3. Plan business analysis approach, stakeholder engagement, and scope definition
  4. Use elicitation techniques to gather requirements from diverse stakeholders
  5. Manage the requirements lifecycle from creation through approval and change
  6. Analyze and model requirements using tools like RACI matrices, BPMN, and use cases
  7. Evaluate solution options and make data-driven recommendations
  8. Create traceability matrices to link requirements through to solution
  9. Conduct stakeholder collaboration and manage scope effectively
  10. Develop professional business analysis documentation and presentations
