Syllabus: Business and IT Fundamentals

IT Business Analyst 40 hours DRAFT

Course Description

This course provides a foundational understanding of business systems, strategy, and analysis, guiding students through key concepts such as business ownership types, market systems, strategic planning, and operational workflows. Learners will explore tools like SWOT, PEST, BPMN, and SDLC frameworks to evaluate opportunities, solve business challenges, and model processes. Emphasizing real-world application through case studies, workshops, and a capstone project, the course equips students with the practical skills needed to analyze, optimize, and support business operations using both strategic and technological approaches.

Summative Assessment: Comprehensive Business Analysis Project

The summative assessment evaluates students' ability to apply core business and IT concepts through a comprehensive business analysis project. Students analyze a hypothetical company scenario, integrating knowledge of business forms, strategy, opportunity evaluation, process modeling, IT infrastructure, and SDLC. The assessment includes case studies, written responses, and practical tasks. A competency-based rubric measures proficiency across seven areas: business concepts, strategies, opportunity evaluation, process analysis, IT infrastructure, process modeling, and SDLC. A minimum passing score of 80% is required.

Learning Outcomes

  1. Identify and compare key business forms and market systems.
  2. Apply strategic frameworks to develop business strategies and SMART goals.
  3. Evaluate business opportunities using analytical tools and decision matrices.
  4. Troubleshoot business challenges using structured problem-solving techniques.
  5. Map workflows and processes, identifying key inputs and outputs.
  6. Create business process models using BPMN and UML.
  7. Explain the components of IT infrastructure and their role in supporting business.
  8. Compare SDLC methodologies and outline development processes.
  9. Synthesize course concepts into a comprehensive business analysis project.

Course Outline

1 Introduction to Business Forms and Market Systems 4 hours
Lesson: Characteristics of Business Forms
  • Features of sole proprietorships, partnerships, corporations, and LLCs
  • Liability, taxation, and scalability considerations
Lesson: Overview of Market Systems
  • Free market economies and mixed economies
  • How market systems affect business decisions
Lesson: Case Study – Comparing Business Forms in Market Systems
  • Compare and contrast two business forms operating in different market systems
Activities
  • Case study comparing business forms across market systems
2 Business Strategies and Goals 4 hours
Lesson: Types of Business Strategies
  • Differentiation, cost leadership, and focus strategies
  • Real-world strategy examples
Lesson: Setting and Evaluating SMART Goals
  • Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, and Time-bound goals
Lesson: Workshop – Developing Business Strategies and SMART Goals
  • Develop a business strategy and SMART goals for a startup concept
Activities
  • Workshop developing business strategies and SMART goals
3 Business Opportunity Evaluation 4 hours
Lesson: Identifying Opportunities
  • SWOT and PEST analysis tools
  • Scanning the environment for opportunities
Lesson: Evaluating Alternatives
  • Decision matrices for assessing options
  • Cost-benefit and feasibility analysis
Lesson: Presenting an Evaluated Business Opportunity
  • Present an evaluated business opportunity to peers
Activities
  • Group presentation of evaluated business opportunity
4 Business Scenario Analysis and Troubleshooting 4 hours
Lesson: Common Business Challenges
  • Typical business problems and their solutions
Lesson: Tools for Analysis
  • Root cause analysis and flowcharts
  • Structured problem-solving techniques
Lesson: Case Analysis – Troubleshooting Business Scenarios
  • Solve a business problem using troubleshooting techniques and present findings
Activities
  • Case analysis solving a business problem
5 Operational Unit Inputs and Outputs 4 hours
Lesson: Tracing Workflows and Processes
  • Mapping workflows and operational processes
Lesson: Input/Output Relationships
  • How inputs and outputs interact in production, sales, and service units
Lesson: Simulation – Creating and Analyzing a Workflow Map
  • Create a workflow map for a production unit and analyze its inputs and outputs
Activities
  • Simulation creating and analyzing a workflow map
6 Business Process Modeling 4 hours
Lesson: Overview of Modeling Languages
  • BPMN (Business Process Model and Notation)
  • UML (Unified Modeling Language)
Lesson: Hands-On Practice
  • Create basic diagrams using modeling tools
Lesson: Design a BPMN Diagram for a Business Workflow
  • Design a BPMN diagram for a selected business workflow
Activities
  • Design a BPMN diagram for a selected business workflow
7 IT Infrastructure Basics 4 hours
Lesson: Components of IT Infrastructure
  • Hardware, software, and networks
Lesson: Role of IT in Business
  • How IT supports business processes and decision-making
Lesson: Discussion – Evaluating IT Infrastructure Needs
  • Evaluate the IT infrastructure needs for a sample business
Activities
  • Discussion evaluating IT infrastructure needs
8 Software Development Lifecycle (SDLC) 4 hours
Lesson: Phases of SDLC
  • Planning, analysis, design, implementation, and maintenance phases
Lesson: Agile vs. Waterfall Methodologies
  • Compare and contrast methodologies
  • Develop an outline of an SDLC process for a simple software project
Activities
  • Develop an SDLC process outline for a software project
9 Capstone Project – Comprehensive Business Analysis 8 hours
Lesson: Capstone Project
  • Integrate all course concepts into a realistic business scenario
  • Develop stakeholder needs analysis, business opportunity evaluation, process models, IT alignment, and solution recommendations
  • Present findings and propose innovative solutions to peers and instructor

Labs & Practical Exercises

  • Case studies comparing business forms and market systems
  • SWOT and PEST analysis workshops
  • Root cause analysis and troubleshooting exercises
  • Workflow mapping simulations
  • BPMN diagram creation using modeling tools
  • IT infrastructure needs evaluation
  • SDLC process development project
  • Comprehensive capstone business analysis and presentation
