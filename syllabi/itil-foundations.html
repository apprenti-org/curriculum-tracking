Syllabus: ITIL Foundations

Operations Support Specialist — Network 20 hours CURRENT

Course Description

ITIL Foundations provides a comprehensive introduction to IT Service Management (ITSM) using the ITIL 4 framework. This course covers core ITIL principles, service design and transition practices, operational support and incident management, and continuous improvement methodologies. Learners will understand how to align IT services with business needs, implement best practices for service delivery, and prepare for the ITIL Foundation certification exam. The course emphasizes practical application of ITIL concepts in real-world operational environments.

Summative Assessment

Learners demonstrate mastery through one of two assessment options:

Option A: Certification-Style Exam

- 40 multiple choice questions aligned to the ITIL 4 Foundation exam format

- 60 minutes, 65% passing score

- Covers all modules: ITSM fundamentals, SVS, guiding principles, value chain, practices, and continual improvement

Option B: Applied Case Study Project

- Analyze a real or fictional IT service organization and:

- Identify value co-creation between provider and consumer

- Map the service value chain activities

- Identify ITIL practices in use

- Recommend improvements using the continual improvement model

- Present findings to the class

Course Outline

1 Introduction to IT Service Management 2 hours
Lesson: 1: What is IT Service Management (ITSM)? (1h)
  • What is IT Service Management and why it matters
  • Products vs services vs value
  • Co-creation of value
  • The service relationship model: service provider, service consumer, and service relationships
  • Customers, users, sponsors, and other stakeholders
  • The four dimensions of service management: organizations and people, information and technology, partners and suppliers, value streams and processes
  • Identify services students use daily (Netflix, Google Drive, LMS)
  • Map "what is the service vs. what is the product?"
Lesson: 2: Overview of the ITIL Framework (1h)
  • History and evolution of ITIL to ITIL 4
  • ITIL 4 structure and certification pathway
  • Key terminology: outcome, output, cost, risk
  • Utility ("fit for purpose") vs warranty ("fit for use") — exam-critical distinction
  • Service offerings: goods, access to resources, service actions
  • How external factors (PESTLE) affect the four dimensions
2 The ITIL Service Value System 3 hours
Lesson: 3: The Service Value System (SVS) (1.5h)
  • What is the Service Value System?
  • Inputs (opportunity and demand) → Outputs → Value
  • Components of the SVS: guiding principles, governance, service value chain, practices, continual improvement
  • How the components interact
Lesson: 4: Governance and the SVS in Action (1.5h)
  • Organizational governance within the SVS
  • How governance directs and controls the organization
  • Applying the SVS to real-world service delivery
  • Diagram a real-world service using SVS (e.g., food delivery app)
3 The 7 Guiding Principles 3 hours
Lesson: 5: Guiding Principles 1–4 (1.5h)
  • Focus on value
  • Start where you are
  • Progress iteratively with feedback
  • Collaborate and promote visibility
Lesson: 6: Guiding Principles 5–7 and Application (1.5h)
  • Think and work holistically
  • Keep it simple and practical
  • Optimize and automate
  • How principles interact and support each other
  • Case study: broken IT system → apply guiding principles
  • Group discussion: which principles are violated?
4 The Service Value Chain 3 hours
Lesson: 7: The 6 Value Chain Activities (1.5h)
  • Plan
  • Improve
  • Engage
  • Design and transition
  • Obtain/build
  • Deliver and support
  • Inputs and outputs of each activity
Lesson: 8: Value Streams and Workflow (1.5h)
  • How activities interconnect to create value streams
  • Inputs and outputs between value chain activities
  • Example value streams: incident resolution, service request fulfillment, new service deployment
  • Mapping workflows through the value chain
  • Identifying bottlenecks and optimization opportunities
  • Map a help desk ticket through the value chain — trace inputs/outputs between activities
  • Map a service request through the value chain
  • Identify bottlenecks and propose improvements
5 ITIL Practices 5 hours
Lesson: 9: Incident Management (1h)
  • Purpose: "To minimize the negative impact of incidents by restoring normal service operation as quickly as possible"
  • Key terms: incident, major incident, incident model
  • Incident lifecycle and prioritization (impact and urgency)
  • Severity-based escalation processes (functional and hierarchical) (OJL 2b)
  • Incident bridges and major incident procedures
  • Runbooks and incident response documentation (OJL 2d)
Lesson: 10: Problem Management (1h)
  • Purpose: "To reduce the likelihood and impact of incidents by identifying actual and potential causes of incidents, and managing workarounds and known errors"
  • Key terms: problem vs incident vs known error — exam-critical distinctions
  • Problem identification and logging
  • Root cause analysis (RCA) techniques (OJL 2c)
  • Known errors and workarounds
  • Relationship between incident and problem management
Lesson: 11: Change Enablement and Release Management (1h)
  • Purpose (change enablement): "To maximize the number of successful service and product changes by ensuring that risks have been properly assessed"
  • Purpose (release management): "To make new and changed services and features available for use"
  • Key terms: change model, change authority, change schedule
  • Types of changes: standard, normal, emergency
  • Impact assessments and mitigation planning for production changes (OJL 4c)
  • Change authority, change advisory board (CAB), and emergency CAB
  • Release governance and deployment planning (OJL 4d)
Lesson: 12: Service Level Management (1h)
  • Purpose: "To set clear business-based targets for service levels, and to ensure that delivery of services is properly assessed, monitored, and managed against these targets"
  • Key terms: SLA, SLO, OLA, service review
  • Service level agreements (SLAs) — structure and content
  • Service level objectives (SLOs) and operational level agreements (OLAs) (OJL 2f)
  • Measuring and reporting service performance against uptime targets
  • Service reviews and customer feedback
Lesson: 13: Service Desk, Service Request Management, and Supporting Practices (1h)
  • Purpose (service desk): "To capture demand for incident resolution and service requests; the single point of contact between the service provider and users"
  • Purpose (service request management): "To support the agreed quality of a service by handling all predefined, user-initiated service requests"
  • Service request fulfillment and approval workflows
  • Supporting practices — purpose and key concepts:
  • Simulate a help desk scenario: incident vs problem vs change
  • Build a simple workflow for handling service requests
6 Continual Improvement 2 hours
Lesson: 14: The Continual Improvement Model (1h)
  • The six steps of the continual improvement model (exam expects recognition in order):
  • How each step connects to the next
  • Memorization drill: order the steps from a shuffled list
  • Quick-fire recall checkpoint
Lesson: 15: Applying Continual Improvement (1h)
  • Improving operational processes and workflows (OJL 5c)
  • Using measurement, metrics, and KPIs to drive improvement
  • Continual improvement register
  • Relationship between continual improvement and the guiding principles
  • Students evaluate a broken process and propose improvements using the model steps in order
7 ITIL in the Real World and Exam Preparation 2 hours
Lesson: 16: ITIL in Practice (0.5h)
  • ITIL in help desk and support roles
  • ITIL in DevOps and Agile environments — how they work together
  • Tools: ServiceNow, Jira Service Management
  • Mapping ITIL concepts to real job postings
Lesson: 17: Comprehensive Review (0.5h)
  • Key concepts and terminology review
  • Practices summary and relationships between practices
  • Common exam pitfalls and traps
Lesson: 18: Practice Exam (0.5h)
  • Exam format: 40 questions, 60 minutes, 65% pass mark
  • Timed practice exam simulation
  • Question analysis and answer strategies
Lesson: 19: Case Study and Final Review (0.5h)
  • Real-world case study applying ITIL practices to a service management scenario
  • Exam day preparation and tips
  • ITIL certification pathway: next steps to ITIL Specialist
  • Role-play: service desk vs developer vs manager
  • Map ITIL concepts to real job postings
